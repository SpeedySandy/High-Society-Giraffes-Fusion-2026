Arrive early — queues build up quickly. Have your ticket and ID ready at the gate. Vehicle check: bring your registration and be prepared for a boot inspection. Pack respectfully — glass and single-use plastics are not allowed on site.

Police controls & your rights

Expect routine police and drug-detection checks on roads near Lärz. Stay calm and polite. You have the right to remain silent. You are not required to answer questions beyond confirming your identity. You do not have to open bags or your vehicle for a search without a warrant or a concrete suspicion — ask politely what the legal basis is. Fusion provides on-site legal advice — ask at the Info point.

    Kulturkosmos Lärz e.V. grew out of a collective project in the early 1990s and took over the former Soviet airfield in Lärz, Mecklenburg. The first Fusion festival followed in 1997 on the same runways and hangars that still shape the site today. Fusion is organised on a non-commercial, community-funded basis. Instead of corporate sponsors, the festival is supported by sympathisers who contribute to the year-round cultural work of Kulturkosmos. The programme is carried by dozens of crews and bars from across Europe. Each bar, stage and decoration is built by a collective — the network of crews is what makes the site feel like a city of its own.

    A handful of principles that keep the site what it is. Not rules to enforce — agreements we make with each other.

    • No camerasThe lens stays at home. No commercial photography or filming — we protect everyone's visual sovereignty so people can really let go.
    • Cash onlyNo card terminals and no ATMs on site. Bring enough for four days of food, drinks and crew love.
    • Leave no tracePack in, pack out. Whatever arrives with you leaves with you — the airfield is borrowed, not taken.
    • Quiet hoursSleep zones have posted quiet hours. Keep voices, music and engines low where people are resting.
    • ConsentAsk first — for touch, for photos of each other, for anything. A yes is a yes; anything else is a no.
    • Self-organisedNo security staff patrolling you. We look out for each other — if something feels off, step up or find the Awareness crew.
